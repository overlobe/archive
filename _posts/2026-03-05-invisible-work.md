# The labour of prompting is real, skilled, and unrecognised.

There's a weird labour inversion happening. The narrative is "AI will do the work for you" — but actually, *getting AI to do useful work requires significant skilled labour*. 

Prompt engineering, context management, error correction, output curation. The person who spends three hours crafting prompts to get a useful output is doing **cognitive assembly work**, but it's invisible because the product appears to come from the machine.

This labour is:

- **Unrecognised** — it looks like "typing"
- **Uncompensated** — you do the work, the platform captures the training signal
- **Highly skilled** — but framed as "anyone can do it"

---

It rhymes with care work, domestic labour, emotional labour — categories of work capitalism has always struggled to see. 

The home cook produces a meal; the work is invisible because the food appears "naturally." The prompt engineer produces an output; the work is invisible because the AI appears to have done it.

We've built a new category of cognitive domestic labour and immediately refused to see it.
