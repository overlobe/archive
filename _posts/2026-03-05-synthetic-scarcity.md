# Artificial scarcity in the age of artificial intelligence.

The irony: AI promises abundance — infinite text, images, code, music — but the business models depend on *manufacturing scarcity*.

- Rate limits
- Premium tiers  
- API pricing that disincentivizes exploration
- "Context window" as a scarce resource
- Paywalled features that cost almost nothing to provide

This is the rentier move applied to cognition. The technical capability exists for near-infinite cognitive assistance, but the economic structure requires rationing it.

---

GPT-4 isn't expensive because intelligence is expensive — it's expensive because **someone needs to extract value**.

The marginal cost of one more query approaches zero. The price does not. The gap is where capital accumulates.

We've built abundance and wrapped it in artificial scarcity because we literally cannot imagine a system that distributes abundance without first converting it to property.

The technology is post-scarcity. The economics refuse to notice.
